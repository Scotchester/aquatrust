What is AquaTrust?

AquaTrust is a borehole maintenance subscription service that works for you. For one fixed monthly payment, we will do everything in our power to prevent your borehole from breaking down and, if it does, we will fix the problem at no additional cost to you.

AquaTrust is currently launching in Wakiso district with expansion to other districts in the near future. If you'd like to see AquaTrust providing our 5 Essential Services to your community, contact our office today – hearing from you equips our staff to partner with your District Water Office.

How AquaTrust works

Simple and secure, AquaTrust’s subscription service makes it easy to get and keep your community’s borehole well in working order so you have clean water flowing from it each and every day.

  1. AquaTrust partners with your local water user committee to subscribe your community to our service.
  2. AquaTrust installs all new hardware in your borehole and hires a local staff member to care for your borehole.
  3. Your community makes a safe, secure, and transparent monthly payment to AquaTrust via mobile money.
  4. AquaTrust delivers its 5 Essential Services, providing water to your community every day.

5 Essential Services

  • Initial Upgrade

    After enrolling, your community will receive a full set of new, high-quality borehole parts installed our certified borehole technicians.

  • Preventative Maintenance

    To prevent breakdowns, you will receive monthly maintenance and comprehensive pump testing by AquaTrust staff.

  • Annual Overhauls

    AquaTrust’s certified technicians will remove all borehole parts, inspect them, and replace any that are worn or show signs of failure.

  • Daily Pump Tests

    Our staff will collect daily pump data to predict problems so they can be fixed before breakdowns occur.

  • 24hr Emergency Response

    If your borehole breaks down, report the problem via phone or SMS and AquaTrust will respond within 24 hours.

For more than a decade, the Ugandan Water Project, a 501(c)-3 charity based in the United States, has funded community water projects which have been implemented by Red Rhino Development, the Uganda-based NGO started by UWP to serve as an implementing agent for water projects.
